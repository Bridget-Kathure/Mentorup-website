Born a crime chapter 1 and 2 summary

Chapter 1 and 2

Trevor Noah's life story is a unique and challenging one, as he grew up in South Africa during the apartheid era.
Born to a black Xhosa mother and a Swiss father in 1984, he encountered various obstacles due to his mixed-race
identity.The apartheid system was used by the Afrikaners to develop racial oppression, driving the native South
Africans from their lands into slavery. The Immorality Act of 1927 outlawed native black-and-white relationships,
punishing men with not less than 5 years and women with 4 years.


Trevor's life was full of danger, and his mom even threw him off a moving bus to save their lives.In their lives,
they are constantly fleeing either from the police officer looking to expose Trevor for being mixed race or Trevor
from his mom to escape beating.Despite all these challenges, Trevor's mother remained a strong,independent woman
with a staunch Christian faith.She never let discrimination hold her back and worked hard to secure a job outside
of the factory.She even dated her white neighbor, Robert, who would become Trevor's father.

Relation To Society

Trevor's experiences highlight the importance of family in life and how it shapes our attitudes and behaviors.
The chapters relating to the recent society highlight themes of social injustices and racial inequalities.
Trevor's story serves as a reminder of the struggles and progress in society's journey toward equality and acceptance.
By acknowledging the importance of family support and perseverance, we can learn from Trevor's experiences and work
towards a more just and fair society.
